Photography Short Film

Aim

This project explores the intricate relationship between the ocean and the people of Shishi District, Fujian. Historically a thriving coastal city with deep roots in the fishing industry, Shishi’s once-abundant marine resources are now under threat. Overfishing, industrial pollution, an unsustainable fishery structure, and lax policy enforcement have contributed to the collapse of the marine ecosystem. As a result, traditional fishing villages are vanishing, gradually replaced by tourism developments, such as luxury hotels. On a spiritual level, the local community continues to uphold ancient religious customs, like burning paper offerings for ancestors. Despite the enduring cultural bond between the city and the sea, these photographs capture a poignant forecast of the extinction of Fujian’s fishing villages and the fragile traditions they have long preserved.
